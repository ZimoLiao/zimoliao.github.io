title: "Turbulence modulation in particle-laden channel flow: the particle inertial effects"
collection: publications
permalink: /publication/2025-01-25-paper-modulation
excerpt: "We explore the statistical features of turbulent channel flow laden with small heavy particles at Stokes number $St^+$ ranging from $3$ to $300$, through four-way coupled point-particle direct numerical simulations. It is clearly elucidated how the increase in the Stokes number of inertial particles leads to a flow transition from a drag-enhanced to a drag-reduced state, before recovering to the single-phase turbulence asymptotically. A comprehensive point-based statistical description of particle-laden turbulence is developed, enabling a profound understanding of interphase coupling and, in turn, identifying the two essential mechanisms through which particles modify the carrying flow directly: particle-induced extra transport and particle-induced extra dissipation. Their competition at various levels -- streamwise momentum transport, kinetic energy transfer, and self-sustaining processes -- is highlighted as the root cause of the subtle flow modulation. Further, inspired by P. G. Saffman's seminal work (Saffman, P. G. 1962. J. Fluid Mech. 13 (1), 120--128), the limit behaviors ($St^+\to0$ or $\infty$) of particles are examined analytically to clarify the particle inertial effects in turbulence modulation."
